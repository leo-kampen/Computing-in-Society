Drawbacks

There are three major drawbacks of automation. Those being job displacements, initiation costs, and security risks.

Job Displacement

When companies decide to automate, there may be job displacements that are geared towards manual labor and repetitive tasks. In order for companies to handle this, there should be upskilling and reskilling to help foster a smooth transition from humans to automation.

Initiation Cost

Automation can come with a substantial initiation cost and can be difficult for some companies to adapt due to this. There may be a need for infrastructure changes, training programs, and the integration of the new technology.

Security Risks

The security risks that come along with automation can lead to some pretty serious problems. As more parts of industries get automated, there is more sensitive information that is being digitized and interconnected. This can lead to many data privacy concerns and security risks.
